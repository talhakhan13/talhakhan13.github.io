About Me

I am an assisstant professor in the College of Computing at Grand Valley State University. I completed my PhD in 2025 from the University of Pittsburgh (Pitt) where I was advised by Jacob Biehl.

My research lies in the general area of human-computer interaction (HCI). I focus on designing mixed reality technologies that create novel ways of interacting with information, with much of this work applied in healtcare surgical settings. I also investigate the challenges of multi-user MR environments and design solutions that enhance collaboration and shared experiences in these spaces. I have published my findings in leading venues such as ACM ISS, ACM CSCW, ACM SUI, and IEEE ISMAR (best paper award winner 2023).
