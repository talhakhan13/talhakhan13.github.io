Hello...

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Information Sciences and Technology at Grand Valley State University, where I lead the Immersive Interaction Lab. I completed my PhD in 2025 from the University of Pittsburgh, where I was advised by Jacob Biehl.

My research lies in the general area of human-computer interaction (HCI). I design mixed reality (MR) systems that enable novel ways of interacting with information. Much of my work explores the application of MR in surgical contexts. At the same time, I study the challenges of incorporating MR in multi-user environments, and develop solutions that enhance both personal and collaborative experiences in these spaces. I have published my findings in leading venues such as ACM ISS, ACM SUI, and IEEE ISMAR, where I received Best Paper Award in 2023.

During my doctoral studies, I co-founded the Surreality Lab at the University of Pittsburgh Medical Center. I also held research internships at the Augmented Perception Lab at Carnegie Mellon University and in the Human-Centered AI division at Toyota Research Institute.
